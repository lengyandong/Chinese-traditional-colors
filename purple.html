Ning Ye Zi（凝夜紫）

The color got its name from a poem called "Song of the Swan Gate"(《雁门太守行》), the poem describes a dark and solemn and stirring purple, now we call it "Ning Ye Zi".

in Chinese
雁门太守行
李贺
黑云压城城欲摧，甲光向日金鳞开。
角声满天秋色里，塞上燕脂凝夜紫。
半卷红旗临易水，霜重鼓寒声不起。
报君黄金台上意，提携玉龙为君死。
in English
Song of the Swan Gate
Li He
Dark clouds over the town threaten to crush it down;Like golden scales the armor glistens in sunlight.
Blowing up to the sky, horns take an autumn dye;Rougelike blood on the frontier turns violet at night.
With red flags half furled up, our troops come near Yi river;Muffled by heavy frost, their drumbeats can't be heard.
To fulfill the sovereign's Golden Tower dream,Jade dragon sword in hand, they'd die without a word.
(Translated by Xu Yuanchong)
